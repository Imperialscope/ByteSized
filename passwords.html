Passwords

1) Analogy: Keys and Locks

A password is like the key to your house. If the key is short and common, it’s easy to copy. A strong, unique key for each door keeps each place safe, even if one key is lost.

2) What Makes a Strong Password?

  • Use a long passphrase (12–16+ characters).
  • Mix words with numbers and symbols you’ll remember.
  • Use a different password for every account.
  • Store them in a password manager so you don’t have to remember all.
  • Turn on two‑factor authentication where possible.

3) Do This Now

1Pick a manager: Bitwarden, 1Password, or your browser’s built‑in manager.

2Create a strong master password: a long, memorable phrase.

3Save logins as you go: let the manager generate unique passwords.

4Update weak ones: change any reused/short passwords you find.
